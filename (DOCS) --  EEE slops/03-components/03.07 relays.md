# 03.07 - Relays

## What is a Relay?

A relay is an electrically operated switch that uses an electromagnet to mechanically control one or more sets of contacts. When current flows through the relay's coil, it creates a magnetic field that attracts a movable armature, opening or closing the contacts. This allows a low-power signal to control a high-power circuit, providing isolation between the control and load circuits.

The relay was invented in 1835 by Joseph Henry, an American scientist who also discovered electromagnetic induction. Relays were essential in early telegraph systems and telephone exchanges. Today, they remain widely used in automotive, industrial control, home appliances, and countless other applications where electrical isolation or high-current switching is needed.

## Basic Relay Operation

### Electromagnetic Principle

A relay works by using a small current to energize an electromagnet, which physically moves mechanical contacts.

### Contact Ratings

```

    Maximum Switching Voltage:
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ The highest voltage the contacts can break safely
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Typical: 30V DC, 250V AC, 125V AC, 240V AC
    в””в”Җв”Җ Exceeding may cause arcing, welding, or fire

    в”ңв”Җв”Җ The highest current the contacts can carry/break
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Typical: 1A, 2A, 5A, 10A, 15A, 30A, 50A

    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Usually same as switching current
    в””в”Җв”Җ For motor loads: derate (inrush current higher)

    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Example: 30V Г— 10A = 300W (DC)
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Example: 250V Г— 10A = 2500VA (AC)

## Measuring and Testing Relays

### Testing Coil

```
COIL TESTING (Multimeter)

    Set to resistance mode (О©)

    Good coil: Reads specified resistance (e.g., 400О©)
    Open coil: Reads "OL" (infinite) вҶ’ replace relay
    Shorted coil: Reads very low (e.g., 10О© for 400О© coil) вҶ’ replace

    Coil resistance varies with temperature:
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Copper has positive temp coefficient (+0.393%/В°C)
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Cold room: lower resistance
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Hot relay: higher resistance
    в””в”Җв”Җ Normal variation Вұ10% acceptable


COIL TESTING (Voltage)

    Apply rated coil voltage (e.g., 12V)
    Listen for audible "click"
    If no click:
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Check voltage at coil terminals
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Check coil resistance
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Check mechanical obstruction
    в””в”Җв”Җ Replace if no click with correct voltage


PULL-IN VOLTAGE:

    Minimum voltage to reliably energize relay
    Typically 70-80% of rated voltage
    Example: 12V relay pulls in at 8-9V

    Drop-out voltage:
    Voltage where relay de-energizes (opens)
    Typically 10-30% of rated voltage
    Example: 12V relay drops out at 1-4V
```

### Testing Contacts

```
CONTACT TESTING (Multimeter)

    Set to resistance mode (О©) or continuity mode

    De-energized relay:
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ NC to COM: Should read <1О© (closed)
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ NO to COM: Should read "OL" (open)
    в””в”Җв”Җ If not: welded contacts (replace)

    Energized relay:
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Apply coil voltage (click should be heard)
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ NC to COM: Should read "OL" (open)
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ NO to COM: Should read <1О© (closed)
    в””в”Җв”Җ If not: burnt contacts (replace)


CONTACT RESISTANCE:

    New/good relay: <0.1О© (100mО©)
    Acceptable: <0.5О©
    Marginal: 0.5-2О© (cleaning may help)
    Failed: >2О© or intermittent (replace)

    High contact resistance causes:
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Voltage drop across contacts
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Heating at contacts
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ Intermittent operation
    в””в”Җв”Җ Premature failure


CONTACT WELDING (Stuck closed):

    Contacts cannot open when coil de-energized
    Caused by: Overcurrent, arcing, DC switching
    Solution: Replace relay (cannot repair)
    Prevention: Use correct rating, add snubber
```

## Common Relay Problems

### Problem 1: Relay Chatters (Rapid Clicking)

```
SYMPTOMS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Relay clicks on and off rapidly (several times per second)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Load turns on/off rapidly (lights flash)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Audible buzzing

CAUSES:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Insufficient coil voltage (below pull-in voltage)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Power supply cannot provide enough current
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Loose connection (intermittent coil power)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Damaged coil (intermittent open)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Mechanical obstruction (armature sticking)

DIAGNOSIS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Measure coil voltage (should be вүҘ rated voltage)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Monitor coil voltage with oscilloscope (look for drop)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Check power supply current capability
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Wiggle connections while listening
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Apply direct battery power (bypass circuit)

SOLUTIONS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Increase coil voltage (use higher voltage supply)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Use larger power supply (higher current)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Tighten connections (screw terminals, solder joints)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Add capacitor across coil (100-1000ВөF, holds voltage)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Use relay driver transistor (not direct from IC)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Replace relay if coil damaged
```

### Problem 2: Contacts Welded (Stuck)

```
SYMPTOMS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Load stays ON even when coil de-energized
в”ңв”Җв”Җ No "click" when coil energized (already closed)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Multimeter shows contacts closed with coil off

CAUSES:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Overcurrent (load exceeds contact rating)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Switching DC without arc suppression
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Motor or lamp inrush current too high
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Short circuit on load side
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Contact material wrong for application

DIAGNOSIS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Remove power, check continuity across contacts
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Contacts should open when relay off
в”ңв”Җв”Җ If stuck closed, relay failed

SOLUTIONS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Replace relay (cannot repair welded contacts)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Use relay with higher contact rating
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Add arc suppression (RC snubber or varistor)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ For DC loads: use diode across load (if possible)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Use solid state relay (no contacts to weld)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Derate relay (use 2Г— rating for inductive loads)

PREVENTION:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Never exceed contact rating
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Add inrush current limiter (NTC thermistor)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Use zero-crossing SSR for AC loads
в”ңв”Җв”Җ For motors: use relay rated for motor load (Г—2-3)
```

### Problem 3: Intermittent Operation

```
SYMPTOMS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Relay works sometimes, fails others
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Tapping relay makes it work/stop
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Vibration affects operation
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Load turns off randomly

CAUSES:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Dirty contacts (oxidation)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Loose connection (coil or contacts)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Cracked solder joint
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Weak spring (aged relay)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Mechanical binding

DIAGNOSIS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Tap relay while monitoring continuity
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Measure contact resistance (should be stable)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Wiggle pins/connections
в”ңв”ңв”Җв”Җ Inspect for cracks or damage
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Test at different temperatures (cold spray, heat gun)

SOLUTIONS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Clean contacts (for large relays, use contact cleaner)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Re-solder connections
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Replace relay if internal
в”ңв”Җв”Җ For dirty contacts: operate relay several times (wipes clean)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Use sealed relay (prevents contamination)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ For low-level signals: use gold-plated contacts

CONTACT CLEANING (Large relays only):
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Remove power
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Use contact cleaning tool (not sandpaper!)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Apply contact cleaner spray
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Operate relay 10-20 times to wipe contacts
в”ңв”Җв”Җ For small relays: replace (cannot access contacts)
```

### Problem 4: Excessive Coil Current

```
SYMPTOMS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Relay coil gets very hot (>60В°C)
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Coil draws more current than specified
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Power supply overloaded
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Relay may fail to release (armature stuck)

в”ңв”Җв”Җ Overvoltage (coil voltage too high)

в”ңв”Җв”Җ Measure coil voltage (compare to rating)

в”ңв”Җв”Җ Measure coil resistance (should match spec)

## Summary

1. **Relay** is an electrically operated switch using an electromagnet

2. **Coil** creates magnetic field when energized; pulls armature to move contacts

3. **Contacts:** COM (common), NC (normally closed), NO (normally open)

4. **SPDT** (Single Pole Double Throw) is the most common configuration

5. **Coil voltage** must match circuit (5V, 12V, 24V, 120V AC, etc.)

6. **Contact rating** must exceed load voltage and current (derate for inductive loads)

7. **Flyback diode** required across coil to protect switching transistor

8. **Contact protection** (RC snubber, varistor) reduces arcing, extends life

9. **Solid State Relay (SSR):** No moving parts, silent, long life, needs heatsink

10. **Latching relay:** Maintains state without coil power (battery saving)

11. **Reed relay:** Very small, fast, sealed contacts, low current

12. **Automotive relay:** 12V, high current (20-70A), sealed, vibration resistant

13. **ULN2003** provides 7 Darlington drivers with built-in flyback diodes

14. **Contact bounce** (1-5ms) can cause multiple switching events (debounce needed)

15. **Never exceed contact ratings** (overcurrent welds contacts)

16. **DC switching** is harder on contacts than AC (arc extinguishes slower)
